Abstract

     
This article covers how nuclear propulsion has come to be developed and its features that take an advantage over other forms of 
      propulsion making it a reliable option for space programs in the next few decade.

Introduction

      As space agencies grow a bigger interest in exploring deeper into space, they must also develop their technology to do so. 
      With travel distances expanding, nuclear propulsion may be the solution engineers have been looking for since they are twice as efficient as chemical rockets meaning they could lower travel times by half (Kotlyar, 2024). 
      Nuclear propulsion simply works by many nuclear reactions generating thrust opposed to chemical rockets where liquid propellants are combusted. 
      This technique can provide high thrust whilst using propellant more efficiently than usual chemical rockets decreasing travel times and increasing payload mass (NASA, 2024). 
      Firstly, an understanding of  how each type of nuclear propulsion system works is discussed followed by a comparison with conventional chemical engines using statistics to outline the benefits of each system.
